Xianjun Yang

Reflection AI

Mountain View, CA

I am a researcher at Reflection AI, working on large language models and AI safety. I completed my PhD in Computer Science at University of California, Santa Barbara, where I was co-advised by Professor Linda Petzold, William Yang Wang, and Stephen Wilson. Prior to joining Reflection AI, I worked at Meta GenAI, where I led the interpretability community of 150+ researchers across FAIR, GenAI, and Reality Labs. I also completed internships at Google and Google DeepMind.

My research focuses on AI Safety, LLM Security and Alignment, Mechanistic Interpretability, and LLM-based Agents. I have published extensively at top-tier venues including ICML, NeurIPS, ACL, and EMNLP, with over 2,400 citations and an h-index of 24. My work has been recognized with awards including the EMNLP 2025 Senior Area Chair Highlights Award and an ICML 2024 Oral presentation.

I was affiliated with UCSB NLP group and UCSB Quantum Foundry during my PhD. Prior to Computer Science, I completed a PhD in Fluid Mechanics at the ME department of UCSB. I received my B.S. in Energy and Power Engineering from Tsinghua University.
