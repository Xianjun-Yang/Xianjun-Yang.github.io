I am a researcher at [Reflection AI](https://reflection.ai), working on large language models and AI safety. I completed my PhD in Computer Science at University of California, Santa Barbara, where I was co-advised by Professor [Linda Petzold](https://engineering.ucsb.edu/people/linda-petzold), [William Yang Wang](https://sites.cs.ucsb.edu/~william/), and [Stephen Wilson](https://materials.ucsb.edu/people/faculty/stephen-wilson). Prior to joining Reflection AI, I worked at Meta GenAI, where I led the interpretability community of 150+ researchers across FAIR, GenAI, and Reality Labs. I also completed internships at Google and Google DeepMind.

My research focuses on **AI Safety**, **LLM Security and Alignment**, **Mechanistic Interpretability**, and **LLM-based Agents**. I have published extensively at top-tier venues including ICML, NeurIPS, ACL, and EMNLP, with over 2,400 citations and an h-index of 24. My work has been recognized with awards including the EMNLP 2025 Senior Area Chair Highlights Award and an ICML 2024 Oral presentation.

I was affiliated with [UCSB NLP group](http://nlp.cs.ucsb.edu/) and [UCSB Quantum Foundry](https://quantumfoundry.ucsb.edu/) during my PhD. Prior to Computer Science, I completed a PhD in Fluid Mechanics at the ME department of UCSB. I received my B.S. in Energy and Power Engineering from Tsinghua University.
